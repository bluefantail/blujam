What's this ECS100/UI thing you keep talking about?

The ECS100 Jar is the ONLY external library permitted by BluJm rules. It is the library given to 100-level students at Victoria University of Wellington Engineering and Computer Science students and is the backbone of this competition.

Why can't I use X language instead of Java

Because for this event we want to focus on junior developers while giving a fun twist. Look out for Pxljm

Why can't I use X library/jar/engine (even though it's open source).

We have a hard rule on submissions not having any external dependencies of any kind. We discourage the use of code not written by your team members as it detracts from the original meaning of the game jam. You can however copy/combine/change your existing code to fit your project, meaning you could create an engine for your team before the competition (in fact, we encourage this). Look out for pxljm

How many people can I have in my team?

We impose a maximum of 4 for your own good. From our experience of doing game jams, rarely does anything good come out of having 5 or more members in a team. We suggest you split your potential members into 2 or more smaller groups if that is the case.

Do you supply Food/Water?

Pizza? Water is freely available at one of the drinking fountains or in one of the bathrooms.

What do I need to bring?

The only thing you are "required" to bring is your entry fee and a computer. Everything else, including food, spare clothes, deodorant, is optional but highly recommended. Please bring headphones if you need sound, there will be ALOT of other people who do not want/need to listen to your bad music or repetitive gameplay testing (Looking at you TAFT).

Do you have a spare computer for me to use?

No. See "What do I need to bring?".

Will we have internet?

Yes, there is guaranteed internet over Guest WiFi at VUW. We will also setup a faster wired internet and WiFi permitting we don't run into technical troubles (again). You will need internet to make your final submission.

Is there (overnight) parking available?

Yes, there is free parking available all weekend, see our event description on where to park. Officially it is available from 7pm Friday until 5am Monday, but if you're lucky you could park there earlier on Friday night. We take no responsibility if you get ticketed before 7pm on the Friday though.

Yes, you can leave and come back anytime, as many times as you want. In the spirit of Game Jams we encourage everyone to spend their time programming at the event, but we also want people to look after themselves, which often includes getting a good night sleep in their own bed instead of on the floor. Why the name BluJm?

It started when we were looking to create another game jam alongside PxlJm. We were focused on encouraging and nurturing newer people while still having the more experienced game makers join in. We decided that by introducing a rule that reduced the tools to those the newer people are familiar with or are easier to grasp they would feel more comfortable creating a game with them. The name BluJm comes from the BlueJ IDE that new ECS students use at VUW use when they learn to program in Java.
